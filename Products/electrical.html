Cable Gland

A cable gland is a device designed to attach and secure the end of an electrical cable to the equipment. A cable gland provides strain-relief and connects by a means suitable for the type and description of cable for which it is designed

Power-Control Cable

A cable is a type of physical medium used to transmit signals, data, or power between devices or locations. It consists of one or more insulated conductors (wires) that are typically made of copper or other conductive materials, enclosed within a protective sheath.

Cable Lug

A cable lug, also known as a cable terminal or cable connector, is a device used to connect a cable to electrical equipment, such as terminals, switches, or other conductive components.

LT-HT Sleves

LT-HT Sleeves refer to sleeves or protective coverings used in electrical and industrial applications to insulate, protect, and label cables, wires, and components. The terms "LT" and "HT" likely refer to "Low-Tension" and "High-Tension," respectively, indicating the range of voltage or current the sleeves are designed to handle.

Switchyard Hardware

Switchyard hardware refers to the various components and equipment used in an electrical substation or switchyard. A switchyard is a critical part of an electrical power system where voltage levels are transformed, controlled, and distributed.

HT Circuit Breaker

A High-Tension (HT) Circuit Breaker is an electrical switching device designed to interrupt or isolate high-voltage currents in power systems, ensuring safety, protection, and control of electrical equipment and networks.

Control Transformer

A control transformer is used to step down voltage for powering control devices like relays and switches in electrical systems, ensuring safe and isolated control circuit operation.

Isolator

Isolators are typically designated with voltage ratings such as 11kV, 33kV, 132kV, and so on, where "kV" stands for kilovolts. These ratings indicate the maximum voltage level that the isolator can safely handle.

Lighting Arrester

A lightning arrester, also known as a surge arrester or lightning diverter, is a protective device used in electrical systems to safeguard equipment and structures from the damaging effects of transient voltage surges, including those caused by lightning strikes.

Capacitor Bank

A capacitor bank is a collection of capacitors connected in parallel or series within an electrical circuit. It's designed to store and release electrical energy quickly. Capacitors are passive electronic components that store electrical charge and energy in an electric field between their plates.

Transformer

A transformer is used to step down voltage for powering control devices like relays and switches in electrical systems, ensuring safe and isolated control circuit operation.

Transformer Accessories

Transformer accessories are additional components and devices that are used in conjunction with transformers to enhance their performance, safety, and functionality. These accessories serve various purposes and are often tailored to specific types of transformers and their applications.

Safety Equipments

Safety equipment refers to a variety of devices and tools designed to protect individuals from electrical hazards and ensure safe working conditions when dealing with electricity. These equipments are essential for preventing electrical shocks, burns, and other injuries that can occur due to exposure to live electrical components.
